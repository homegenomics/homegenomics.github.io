Why the polished report from a testing company isn't enough — and what you gain by analyzing it yourself.

## 1. The report shows only part of it

Commercial reports interpret only a handful of variants the company chose. But raw data holds hundreds of thousands to millions of variants. Handling it yourself lets you see what the report left out.

## 2. Data sovereignty

Your genome is your most sensitive personal data — and **data your family shares**. Storing and analyzing the raw data yourself keeps control in your hands, not on a third-party server.

## 3. Protect your family

Relatives share much of their genome. A meaningful variant found in one person may apply to parents, children, and siblings too. The ability to analyze is the ability to **catch risk early, at family scale**.

## 4. Reproducibility

Analyzing the way developers already work — scripts, version control, pipelines — means you can reproduce results, re-run them on new data, and verify them.

## Be clear about the limits

- Doing it yourself **is not a diagnosis.** Clinical judgment belongs to professionals.
- Variant interpretation carries real uncertainty. Beware of over-reading.
- Sensitive data brings **security and privacy** responsibilities.
